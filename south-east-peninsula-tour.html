South East Peninsula Tour

Scenic drive to Timothy Hill, Friars Bay and Cockleshell — the most photogenic stretch of St Kitts for cruise shore excursions.

Cruise Passenger Snapshot

Duration 4–5 hours
Activity Level Easy
Type Shared group
Port Pickup Port Zante
Scenic Value Excellent
Return Confidence High

Why Cruise Passengers Choose This Tour

The south east peninsula is widely considered the prettiest part of St Kitts. Cruise passengers choose this tour for the iconic Timothy Hill viewpoint — where the Atlantic and Caribbean appear to meet — plus white-sand beaches at Cockleshell Bay and Friars Bay with Nevis rising in the background.

What to Expect

  • Scenic coastal drive from Basseterre along the peninsula isthmus
  • Photo stop at Timothy Hill overlook
  • Beach time at Cockleshell Bay, Friars Bay or South Friars
  • Optional visit to a beach bar or reggae beach club
  • Return to Port Zante before your ship's departure

Good to Know Before Booking

  • Beach chairs and umbrellas may cost extra at beach clubs
  • Peninsula roads can be busy on multi-ship days — allow buffer time
  • Some tours include a drink or snack; confirm inclusions
  • Always check your cruise ship arrival and departure times before booking

What to Bring

  • Swimwear, towel and reef-safe sunscreen
  • Cash for beach rentals, food and drinks
  • Camera for Timothy Hill panoramic shots
  • Cover-up or light clothing for the coach

Best For

  • Photographers and scenery lovers
  • Beach enthusiasts wanting iconic St Kitts locations
  • Couples and families seeking a relaxed port day
  • Social media travellers — Timothy Hill is a signature shot

Who Should Choose a Different Tour

  • History-first visitors — try Brimstone Hill or island highlights
  • Snorkel-focused travellers — a catamaran cruise offers more time in the water
  • Railway enthusiasts — book the Scenic Railway
  • Active hikers — consider the rainforest tour
